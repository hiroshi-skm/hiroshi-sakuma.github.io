Hiroshi Sakuma

Born in 1996. At Osaka University, he conducts research on Virtual Beings and Future Society Design. As the director of the Osaka Pavilion at the 2025 Osaka-Kansai Expo, he plans to exhibit “Future Virtual Beings.” He also serves as the principal investigator for research on future society design, a joint project between Osaka University and the Japan Science and Technology Agency. In 2021, he led a team for exploratory research under the Moonshot R&D program. Currently, he holds roles such as Chair of the Shape New World Committee at the Osaka Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Chair of the Task Force on Future Generations in the Japanese Society for Artificial Intelligence, and member of the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of Japan. He received the Japan Open Innovation Award from the Minister of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology in 2022 and was selected for Forbes JAPAN 30 UNDER 30 in 2023.

Biography

Specially Appointed Researcher at the Osaka University Social Solution Initiative; Director of the Osaka Pavilion Promotion Committee for the 2025 World Expo (Osaka-Kansai Expo); Representative of the Shape New World Initiative from the World Economic Forum; Doctoral candidate at the Graduate School of Engineering Science, Osaka University; Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) Research Fellow (DC1); Board Member of FUKAIKA; Member of the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of Japan.

Graduated from the Department of Systems Science, Faculty of Engineering Science, Osaka University (Advisor: Hiroshi Ishiguro) and the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, Department of Multidisciplinary Sciences, The University of Tokyo (Advisor: Takashi Ikegami). During his time at Osaka University, he participated in a one-year exchange program at the Faculty of Engineering Science at the University of Toronto and completed a six-month internship at Panasonic Silicon Valley Lab (at the time).

Recipient of awards such as the Japanese Open Innovation Award from the Minister of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology; the Encouragement Award from the Department of Multidisciplinary Sciences, Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, The University of Tokyo; the Special Award of the 19th Osaka University President’s Award for Extracurricular Activities; the Encouragement Award from the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO) for Excellent Students; and the AI Technology Consortium Project Award 2022.

Selected as a full scholarship recipient at the Masayoshi Son Foundation, a scholarship recipient of the Tobitate! Study Abroad JAPAN program, a member of the World Economic Forum Global Shapers Community, a Forbes JAPAN 30 UNDER 30 honoree in 2023, and featured in NewsPicks Magazine as one of “Seven Under 30 Shaping the Future.”

Currently, he serves as the principal investigator for the joint research project “Shape New World Project,” conducted by the Osaka University Social Solution Initiative and the Research Institute of Science and Technology for Society (RISTEX) under the Japan Science and Technology Agency (JST). He also chairs the “Shape New World Committee,” an expert panel organized by the Shape New World Initiative and the Osaka Chamber of Commerce and Industry.

Other roles include: committee member of the VPIA Community for data utilization at the 2025 Japan World Expo Association (Osaka-Kansai Expo); chair of the working group for startups and international exchange in the Minami Osaka REBORN Consortium by Nikkei Inc.; supervisor of the urban-integrated metaverse “Virtual Osaka”; chair of the Future Generations Task Force and student editorial board chair of the editorial committee at the Japanese Society for Artificial Intelligence; and advisor for the Common Ground Living Lab, among others.

In the past, he has served as the team leader for the Millennium Program under the Japan Science and Technology Agency (JST) Moonshot R&D Project; visiting researcher at the Osaka University Global Initiative; advisor for the Osaka Prefecture Expert Council on the Development of New Strategies; committee member and chair of the Virtual Osaka Pavilion (tentative name) subcommittee in the Expert Council on Local Exhibitions for the Osaka-Kansai Expo organized by Osaka Prefecture and Osaka City; member of the 2025 Osaka-Kansai Expo Advisory Council for the Osaka Chamber of Commerce and Industry; committee member of the Industry Collaboration Committee at the Japanese Society for Artificial Intelligence; organizer of the Creative Destruction Salon at the Osaka Innovation Hub and Osaka Business Development Agency; and curator for Study: Osaka Kansai International Arts Festival. He has curated exhibitions such as Augmented Music and Coexisting Music.

In addition to collaborative projects and research with private companies, universities, and local governments, he has participated in events hosted by economic organizations such as the Osaka Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Kyoto Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Kansai Association of Corporate Executives, and Kyoto Association of Corporate Executives. He has also appeared at events like the JAPAN MOBILITY SHOW, INNOVATION GARDEN, SOCIAL INNOVATION WEEK SHIBUYA, Interop Tokyo, Kansai Future Summit, and Metaverse Japan Summit.

His work has been featured in media outlets such as Nikkei Shimbun, Asahi Shimbun, Yomiuri Shimbun, Sankei Shimbun, Mainichi Shimbun, Kansai TV, MBS, ABEMA Prime, NewsPicks, Gendai Business, WIRED, BRUTUS, CINRA, AXIS Web Magazine, Real Sound, and CNET Japan.

He is a student member of ACM (Association for Computing Machinery), the Japanese Society for Artificial Intelligence, and the Information Processing Society of Japan.
